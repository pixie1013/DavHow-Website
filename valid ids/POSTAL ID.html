PhilPost POSTAL ID

The PhilPost POSTAL ID is a legal primary identification issued by the state-owned Philippine Postal Corporation. It is usually used for postal transactions, banking, and in general, a verification of an identity. The improved version of the Postal ID features a biometrics-based uniqueness tests that ensures its identity. For Filipinos who are foreign residents with Special Retireeâ€™s Resident Visa (SRRV), its validity lasts for three (3) years while foreign residents may enjoy its benefits for one (1) year.

REQUIREMENTS

  • Two (2) copies of duly-accomplished PID Application Form
  • PROOF OF IDENTITY - Submit any one (1) of the following:
    • Birth Certificate Issued by NSO or Local Civil Registry
    • GSIS or SSS UMID Card
    • Valid Driverâ€™s License
    • Valid Passport
    • Marriage Contract for Married Women (Only if documents and IDs submitted do not bear the married name)
  • PROOF OF ADDRESS - Submit any one (1) of the following:
    • Barangay Certificate of Residency issued within three (3) months prior to PID application
    • Certified True Copy of Lease
    • Certified True Copy of Titles issued by the Land Registration Authority (LRA)
    • Certified True Copy of Real Estate Tax Receipt
    • Bank Statement
    • Credit Card Statement
    • School Billing Statement
    • Utility Bill (cable, electric, internet, landline, telephone, water)

    • Certification from hotel, transient home, or any temporary residence within three (3) months prior to application

PROCEDURE

  1. Fill out the application form.
  2. Visit the nearest ID capturing station in Davao City and submit your requirements.
  3. After completing the necessary steps, the identity card will be delivered within twenty (20) working days to your mailing address.
RENEWAL
  • CASE 1: RENEWAL OR REPLACEMENT
    • Two (2) copies of an accomplished PID application form
    • Original and photocopy of Postal ID card
  • CASE 2: LOSS OR THEFT of POSTAL ID
    • Two (2) copies of an accomplished PID application form
    • Affidavit of Loss
  • CASE 3: CHANGE IN BIOGRAPHIC DATA
    • Two (2) copies of an accomplished PID application form
    • Original and a copy of Postal ID
    • Supporting document for change in biographic data
