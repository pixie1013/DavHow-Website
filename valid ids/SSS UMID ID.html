Social Security System (SSS) UMID ID

Like the Government Service Insurance System (GSIS), the Social Security System (SSS) also issues a valid ID for all GSIS, SS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG Fund transactions. The card does not have an expiry date, making it the most frequently used ID in the Philippines.

REQUIREMENTS

  • Duly accomplished UMID application form
  • Any of the following primary valid IDs:
    • Driver’s License
    • Passport
    • Social Security 2D Barcode ID Card
    • Philippine System Identification Card or National ID
    • Seaman’s Book
    • Voter's ID
    • Alien Certificate of Registration
    • Firearm Registration
    • NBI Clearance
    • Postal ID
  • If none of the mentioned IDs are available, bring two (2) copies of IDs/documents with your signature and your photo.

RENEWAL

  • Two (2) valid IDs
  • Affidavit of Loss (if stolen)
  • UMID Card (if damaged)
  • UMID Card Application Form

PROCEDURE

  1. Print and complete the Unified Multi-Purpose ID(UMID) Card Application form. Applicants who have applied for SS numbers online from December 10, 2020, do not have to fill out a UMID application form and only present their SS number application.
  2. Contact or inquire at the nearest SSS branch if they process UMID applications.
  3. Schedule an appointment either online or offline.

    4. ONLINE APPOINTMENT

    1. Visit the official website of SSS.
    2. Log in using your User ID and password on the Member tab.
    3. On the Member Info tab, choose Appointment System.
    4. Complete the necessary details and select your desired schedule.
    5. Take a screenshot or print the appointment details.

    BRANCH WALK-IN APPOINTMENT
    Filipinos who do not have any My.SSS account can schedule appointments by visiting the nearest SSS branch with an enrollment facility.
    Note that the SSS branches implement the Branch Number Coding system. The following are the schedules based on the 10th digit of the SS or Employer (ER) ID.

    10th Digit Corresponding Transaction Day
    1 and 2 Monday
    3 and 4 Tuesday
    5 and 6 Wednesday
    7 and 8 Thursday
    9 and 0 Friday

  4. Visit your nearest SSS office, submit your completed application form and present a valid ID.
  5. After assessing your documents, the applicant's biometrics information, signature, and photos will be requested.
  6. Claim your card within thirty (30) days upon notice of the SSS through SMS at the bank branch you wrote in your application if you applied for a UMID Card as an ATM card or at the SSS branch the applicant applied.

RENEWAL

  1. Accomplish the application form.
  2. Fill up the Miscellaneous Payment Form.
  3. Provide an Affidavit of Loss from the Notary Public office or Public Attorney's Office near you.
  4. Submit your requirements on any SSS branch.
  5. Pay for the replacement fee.
  6. Comply with the remaining processes needed such as data capture.
  7. An Acknowledgement Stub will be given and the replacement will be delivered within three (3) months from the application.
