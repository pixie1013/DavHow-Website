Tax Identification Number Identification Card (TIN ID)

The Tax Identification Number Identification Card (TIN ID) is a valid document issued to Filipino taxpayers, including unemployed citizens or students. According to the country's Tax Code, anyone required to file a tax return or copy shall be given a TIN ID. This document is also free and does not have an expiry. However, a new TIN ID is issued when taxpayers' personal information is changed or updated. The issuance of TIN ID varies from its purpose, which may include individuals and companies, this includes:

  • FOR SELF-EMPLOYED AND MIXED-INCOME INDIVIDUALS
  • ESTATES(under judicial settlement)
  • TRUSTS
  • INDIVIDUALS EARNING PURELY COMPENSATION INCOME
  • NON-RESIDENT CITIZENS
  • OCWs/SEAMEN EARNING PURELY FOREIGN-SOURCED INCOME
  • CORPORATIONS/PARTNERSHIPS(Including Government-Owned and -Controlled Corporations) (Taxable /Non-Taxable)
  • NONRESIDENT FOREIGN CORPORATIONS
  • GOVERNMENT AGENCIES AND INSTRUMENTALITIES
  • LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS
  • ONE-TIME TAXPAYERS (With No Previously Issued TIN)
  • PERSONS REGISTERING UNDER E.O. 98 (securing a TIN to transact with any government office, e.g. LTO, NBI, DFA, etc.)

PROCEDURE

    1. FOR SELF-EMPLOYED AND MIXED INCOME INDIVIDUALS
      • For individual business taxpayers who prefer to submit online, you may visit the BIR New Business Registration Portal and comply with the necessary documents.
      WALK-INS
        1. Submit your documentary requirements together with the BIR Form 1901 version 2018 at the New Business Registrant Counter.
        2. Pay the Annual Registration Fee (P500.00), loose DST (P30.00), and/or payment for the BIR Printed Receipt/Invoice (if the taxpayer opted to buy for use).
        3. A Certificate of Registration (Form 2303) together with the “Notice to Issue Receipt/Invoice”, Authority to Print, BIR Printed Receipts/Invoices (if applicable), and eReceipt will be issued as a proof of payment.
    2. ESTATES (under judicial settlement) & TRUSTS
      1. Submit your accomplished BIR FORM 1901 together with the other requirements to the Revenue District Office.
      2. Pay the Annual Registration Fee (P500.00) at the Authorized Agent Banks of the RDO
    3. INDIVIDUALS EARNING PURELY COMPENSATION INCOME
      1. Submit the BIR Form 1902 and the other requirements to your employer.
      2. Some sections of the application form must be accomplished by the employer.
      3. Submit BIR Form 1902 to the Revenue District Office (RDO) having jurisdiction over the place of office of the employer where such employee is expected to report for work within ten (10) days from the date of employment.
    4. OCWs/SEAMEN EARNING PURELY FOREIGN-SOURCED INCOME
      1. Fill out the BIR Form 1904.
      2. Submit the accomplished BIR Form 1904 on the nearest RDO from the residence of the applicant.
    5. CORPORATIONS/PARTNERSHIPS (Including Government-Owned and Government-Controlled Corporations) (Taxable /Non-Taxable)

      For non-individual business taxpayers who prefer to submit online, you may visit the BIR New Business Registration (NewBizReg) Portal and comply with the necessary documents. (NewBizReg Portal)

      1. Fill out the BIR Form 1903 - Application for Registration for Corporations/ Partnerships (Taxable/Non-Taxable).
      2. Submit the accomplished BIR Form 1903 together with the necessary documents on the nearest RDO from the residence of the applicant.
      3. Pay the following at the New Business Registrant Counter in the BIR Office:
        1. Annual Registration Fee (P500.00);
        2. Documentary Stamp Tax (P30.00) (if applicable);
        3. BIR Printed Receipt/Invoice (if opted to buy for use).
      4. A Certificate of Registration (Form 2303), “Notice to Issue Receipt/Invoice”, Authority to Print, BIR Printed Receipts/Invoices (if applicable), and eReceipt will be issued to the applicant as proof of the transaction.
    6. GOVERNMENT AGENCIES, LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS AND INSTRUMENTALITIES
      1. Fill out the accomplished BIR Form 1903.
      2. Submit the accomplished BIR Form 1903 and the required documents to the nearest RDO from the registered address of the government agencies and instrumentalities or local government units.
    7. NONRESIDENT FOREIGN CORPORATIONS
      1. Visit the BIR Online Registration and Update System (ORUS)
      2. Create an account and complete the online application form.
      3. Scan and upload the necessary documents.
      4. Within three (3) working days, an email notification of the status of the application will be sent regarding the status of the application.
    8. ONE TIME TAXPAYERS (With No Previously Issued TIN)
      1. Fill out the BIR Form 1904.
      2. Submit the BIR Form 1904 and the required documents to the Revenue District Office of the applicant.
    9. PERSONS REGISTERING UNDER E.O. 98 (securing a TIN to be able to transact with any government office, e.g. LTO, NBI, DFA, etc.)
      1. Fill out the BIR Form 1904.
      2. Submit the BIR Form 1904 and the required documents to the Revenue District Office of the applicant.
